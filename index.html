Thinking across disciplines, borders, and lenses

I'm Sarah Seungyoon Kim — a photographer and researcher based between South London and The Hague. I study Global Health and Innovation Society at Leiden University College, with a focus on AI governance and technology policy.

My photography grew out of an interest in how cities are built and experienced — the relationship between structure, light, and human scale. I'm drawn to architecture that asserts itself, and landscapes that ask you to slow down.

Outside photography, I work on questions about how AI governance frameworks developed in the West apply — or fail to apply — in global health contexts, particularly in lower-income settings.
